About This Project

Ray Bradbury gave this advice in 1980, on a panel at Sherwood Oaks Experimental College: read one short story, one poem, and one essay every day for 1,000 days. He said by the end of it you'd be full of enough images, ideas, and feelings to get you writing, and we believe him.

We built this site to support writers, giving them one story, one poem, and one essay, every day. Everything is public domain and hand-curated. The same three pieces go out to every reader on a given day.

The streak counter goes up when you read and resets when you don't. Bradbury never mentioned that part, but it's a helpful motivator.

Built by Devon Lukasiewicz and Joe Mandozzi.

All works are sourced from Project Gutenberg, Standard Ebooks, and PoetryDB—sources that have already verified US public-domain status. If you would like to support this project, please consider donating to these organizations.
